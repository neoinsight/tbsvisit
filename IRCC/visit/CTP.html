Transit through Canada without a visa (China Transit Program)

Who can apply?

You may qualify to transit through Canada without a visa as part of the China Transit Program (CTP)if you are travelling from/to the United States and you:

  • are a citizen and have a valid passport issued by the People’s Republic of China;
  • have a valid United States (U.S.) visa;
  • travel to Canada on one of these airlines:
    • Air Canada
    • Air Canada Rouge
    • Air China
    • WestJet
    • Cathay Pacific
    • China Southern
    • Philippines Airlines
    • Jazz Air
    • Sky Regional Airlines Inc.
    • Air Georgian
    • Hainan Airlines
  • transit through one of these Canadian airports:
    • Vancouver International Airport
    • Calgary International Airport, or
    • Terminal 1 of Toronto’s Pearson International Airport. (Note: If you have to change terminals in Toronto’s Pearson International Airport, you don’t qualify for the CTP)

Travelling to the United States

If you are travelling to the United States you also need to:

  • have a confirmed onward ticket for a direct flight to the United States
  • leave Canada the same travel day (no layover or overnight stay is permitted)
  • board your flight to Canada from one of the following cities:
    • Beijing
    • Guangzhou
    • Shanghai
    • Hong Kong
    • Manila
    • Taipei
    • Tokyo
    • Osaka
    • Nagoya
    • Seoul (South Korea)
    • Xiamen
    • Fuzhou
    • Chengdu
    • Shenyang
    • Harbin
  • arrive during the hours of operation of the United States (U.S.) in-transit pre-clearance facility.

Missing a flight connection or arriving after hours

If you miss your connecting flight or arrive after hours when the U.S. in-transit pre-clearance facility is closed you must go to the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) area and see an officer. The officer will ask you some questions and may decide to give you a temporary resident permit so you can wait for your next flight.

Travelling from the United States

If you are coming from the United States you also need to:

  • arrive in Canada from the United States on a direct, non-stop flight
  • go to the CBSA area for clearance or stay on the transit area while waiting for your connecting flight (passengers that leave the transit area you have to go to the CBSA area for examination)
  • not be under a removal or deportation order
  • have a valid U.S. visa when you entered the United States and not have overstayed in the United States
  • have the travel documents you need to enter your country of destination, and any other countries you are transit through; and
  • have a confirmed onward ticket for a flight that will leave Canada on the same travel day (no layover or overnight stay is permitted).
